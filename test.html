Summary of Pakistan Contract Law (Contract Act, 1872)

Understanding the Contract Act, 1872 (Pakistan)

A simple guide to the legal requirements for a valid and enforceable contract between dealers and contractors, as defined by Pakistani Law.

Essential Elements of a Valid Contract

According to Section 2(h) of the Contract Act, 1872, "An agreement enforceable by law is a contract." For an agreement to be legally binding, all the following elements must be present:

1. Proper Offer and Acceptance

  • One party must make a clear offer, and the other party must accept it fully and without conditions.
  • Communication of the offer and acceptance is crucial.

2. Lawful Consideration ("Something in Return")

  • There must be an exchange of value (money, goods, services, or a promise).
  • The consideration must be real and lawful (e.g., payment for legal services is valid; payment for illegal activity is not).

3. Capacity to Contract (Competent Parties)

  • Both parties must be legally competent to enter an agreement.
  • Parties must be:
    • Of the age of majority (18 years old in Pakistan).
    • Of sound mind at the time of agreement.
    • Not disqualified from contracting by any specific law.

4. Free Consent

  • Agreement must be made voluntarily. Consent is not "free" if obtained by coercion, fraud, misrepresentation, or undue influence.
  • A contract signed under pressure can be considered voidable by the affected party.
